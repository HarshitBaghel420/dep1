## 6. SUMMARY & KEY INSIGHTS FOR NEXT EVENT

### What Worked
- Coordinator performance was generally appreciated
- Chess event was well-received and uniquely engaging
- Table Tennis had high satisfaction scores
- Logo Design (Logo Locha) participants were happy overall
- High return intent from satisfied participants (5/5 rating = 16 people)

### What Failed
- **Internet/WiFi** — #1 complaint across all gaming events; non-functional or extremely slow
- **Food** — Cold, stale, led to at least 2 reported food poisoning cases
- **Timing** — Events started very late (9AM scheduled → 1PM actual start for some)
- **COD** — Only 2 participants registered; event barely ran
- **BGMI format** — Only 1 match before finals; participants wanted more rounds
- **Venue** — Specific rooms (C005) were too small and overcrowded

### Recommendations for Tech Fusion 3.0 / Next Event
1. **Arrange dedicated internet infrastructure** — minimum 2 routers with tested connectivity before event day
2. **Partner with a food sponsor** (like Om Sweets) to ensure quality, fresh food
3. **Fix event timing** — strict start times, volunteer briefing in advance
4. **Increase COD marketing** or replace with higher-demand game (e.g., Valorant, mentioned by a participant)
5. **Better venue allocation** — larger rooms for gaming events with 20+ participants
6. **Increase BGMI match rounds** — at least 3 qualifying matches before finals
7. **Bigger event = more external universities** — 15% of participants were external; scale up outreach
